  • Stout
    Origin: English
    • Typical Alcohol volume: 4 to 16%

    • Styles: Sweet, Cream, Irish, Imperial, American

    • Often contain roasted flavours and notes of coffee

    • Stout meaning "proud" or "brave", after the 14th century took on the connotation of "strong". The first known use of the word in referance for beer was documented in 1677 in the Egerton Manuscript. The expression stout porter was used in the 1700s for strong versions of porter. Stout would eventually only be associated with porter, becoming a synonym for dark beer.

    IPA
    Origin: English
    • Typical Alcohol volume: 4.5 to 20%

    • Styles: English, American, West Coast

    • Hoppy beer within the broader Pale Ale category

    • IPA or “India Pale Ale.” Originating in England the beer was said to be made with enough hops to preserve the beer from England all the way to India.

    PILSNER
    Origin: Czech
    • Typical Alcohol volume: 4.5% to 5%

    • Styles: Bohemian (Czech), German, American

    • Low to moderate carbonation with pale golden yellow colour, and malty flavours and aromas.

    • Invented in the 19th century in the Czech town of Pilsen. Originally so revolting the people of Pilsen poured away 30 kegs in front of the city hall. Then, built a new brewery (Pilsner Urquell) and hired the Bavarian Josef Groll. Josef used the methods learnt from his father along with new brewing methods, and created a light coloured malt resulting in a golden-yellow beer.
